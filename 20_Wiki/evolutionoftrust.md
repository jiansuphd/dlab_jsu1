# Evolution of Trust

**Location:** `10_Projects/evolutionoftrust/`

## Overview
"The Evolution of Trust" is an interactive guide to the game theory of trust, based on the Iterated Prisoner's Dilemma. It explores why, how, and under what conditions people trust each other by simulating different strategies (such as Copycat, Always Cooperate, Always Cheat) and observing how they interact over time.

## Features
- Interactive trust game and simulation.
- Visualizes the mathematics of cooperation and betrayal.

## Academic Application
This simulation is highly relevant for courses in:
- **Game Theory & Economics:** Understanding the Prisoner's Dilemma, non-zero-sum games, and strategic decision making.
- **Sociology & Psychology:** Exploring human behavior, social dynamics, and the conditions required for trust to evolve in a society.
- **Political Science & Philosophy/Ethics:** Analyzing cooperation, conflict, and the societal impact of miscommunication.
